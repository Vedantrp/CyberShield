Deepfake Verification

Is that really your boss or family member? Use this guide to verify "urgent" voice notes and video calls.

üé• Video Call Verification

AI can swap faces in real-time, but it struggles with physics.

1

The "Hand Wave" Test

Ask the caller to wave their hand slowly in front of their face. Deepfake algorithms often glitch when objects pass over the "face mask".

2

Profile Turn

Ask them to turn their head 90 degrees to the side. 2D-based AI models often fail to render the side profile or ear correctly.

3

Light Source Consistency

Check if the lighting on their face matches the background. AI often generates flat or inconsistent lighting.

üó£Ô∏è Audio/Voice Verification

AI voice clones are common in "kidnapping" or "wire transfer" scams.

1

The "Safe Word" Protocol

Establish a secret code word with your family/team offline. If they assume urgency but don't know the word, it's a scam.

2

Emotional Incongruence

Deepfakes often have a flat tone even when saying "I'm in danger!" Listen for robotic pauses or lack of breathing sounds.

3

Call Them Back

Hang up and call their known number. AI scammers usually use VoIP numbers, not the actual person's line.
